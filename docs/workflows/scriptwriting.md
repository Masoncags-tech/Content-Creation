# Scriptwriting Workflow

## The Job

Write scripts that create a filmed moment, not a voiceover.

The script should answer:

- Who is watching?
- What do they recognize instantly?
- What physically happens before the pitch?
- What is the one line they remember?
- Why would they send it to a friend?

## Script Shape

Use short lines. One spoken idea per shot.

Good:

> I missed the call. Clover didn't.

Bad:

> Clover Digital is an AI-powered automation platform that helps small businesses streamline operations and improve workflow efficiency.

## Required Fields

Every script needs:

- `viewer`
- `pain`
- `format`
- `cold_open_visual`
- `physical_hook`
- `proof_insert`
- `payoff_line`
- `share_reason`

## Writing Rules

- Put the pain before the product.
- Make the first line spoken because of something in the scene.
- Avoid explaining AI.
- Avoid long benefit stacks.
- Use one concrete workflow: follow-up, intake, scheduling, invoice, reminder, report.
- Give the performer a real action while speaking.
- Map every line to a visible action, reaction, cut, or insert.
- Give the viewer one idea, one transformation, and one payoff.
- Introduce the proposition by second three without sounding like a pitch deck.

## 15-Second Beat Map

| Time | Beat | Job |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `0.0-1.5s` | Visual hook | Object, face, hand, interruption, or physical mistake already in motion. |
| `1.5-3.0s` | Premise lock | One sentence or visual tells the viewer why this matters. |
| `3.0-7.0s` | Escalation | One action sharpens the problem, joke, or useful tension. |
| `7.0-11.5s` | Proof/payoff | Show the result, reveal, transformation, or joke. |
| `11.5-15.0s` | Button | Reaction, loop, clean CTA, or memorable character beat. |

## Reusable Script Patterns

- Consequence first: "I thought [old behavior] was fine until [specific consequence]."
- Problem in motion: show the broken thing before explaining it.
- Mistake correction: "Stop doing [mistake]. Do [better move]."
- Status reveal: a normal scene reveals stakes through one prop, screen, or reaction.
- POV pressure: "POV: you have [short time] to fix [specific problem]."
- Micro-transformation: bad state, one action, after state.
- Expert contradiction: "Everyone says [common advice]. The real problem is [truth]."
- Product invisibly saves the day: the product appears as behavior, not a pitch.
- Character truth: a funny workplace/personality truth escalates into action.
- Proof sandwich: claim, visible proof, human reaction.

## Brand Voice Modules

Keep the workflow general, then load brand-specific words only at the end.

Every brand voice module should define:

- words to use
- words to avoid
- emotional lane
- proof style
- claim boundaries
- character or speaker style

### Clover Example

Clover should feel practical, warm, and sharp.

Use:

- digital employee
- follow-up
- booked
- handled
- calendar
- intake
- quote
- missed call
- clean handoff

Avoid:

- AI agent
- chatbot
- revolutionary
- disruptive
- autonomous everything
- platform synergy language

## Creative Bar

The script is not ready until the muted version still has a story.
