# Character Account Mechanics

## Purpose

Use this to study high-performing virtual, mascot, AI, and character-led accounts without copying their IP, designs, voices, or catchphrases.

The goal is transferable grammar:

- recurring world
- consistent character behavior
- dense premise
- physical hook
- native audio
- motivated props
- payoff worth sharing

## Reference Signals

### Nobody Sausage

Source: https://www.nobodysausage.com/

Transferable lesson:

- simple readable character
- repeatable silhouette
- trend-native participation
- brand-collab friendly world
- broad emotional lane: playful, weird, escapist

Do not copy:

- character design
- dance choreography
- exact visual language

### Granny Spills

Source: https://time.com/7329699/ai-influencers-tiktok-granny-spills/

Transferable lesson:

- character voice is specific and sharp
- social tension drives shareability
- recurring persona matters more than photorealism
- production scale still needs human taste and review

Risk:

- authenticity backlash
- platform unoriginality flags
- jokes that rely only on shock can fatigue fast

### EliteBallKnowledge / Luca Maxim

Source: https://www.eliteballknowledge.ai/

Transferable lesson:

- lore-commerce works when products feel like artifacts from the world
- recurring jokes, characters, and status symbols create insider language
- content can sell without feeling like a hard ad when the product is native to the story

Do not copy:

- characters
- names
- lore
- visual motifs

## Transferable Structures

- Character gives intense advice about a normal problem.
- Startup/workplace scene goes wrong in one specific way.
- POV: the mascot discovers a business truth.
- Fake documentary cutaway with dead-serious narration.
- Product or service proof appears as an in-world consequence.
- Recurring catchphrase is attached to original lore.
- Character reacts to a trend through its own worldview.
- One object causes the whole scene.

## Character Consistency

Define before production:

- silhouette
- emotional lane
- vocal texture
- default posture
- default setting
- recurring prop
- social status
- what the character wants
- what the character misunderstands
- what the character is weirdly good at

If the character could be swapped with any other character and the scene still works, the format is not strong enough.

## Premise Density

A 15-second character clip gets:

- one premise
- one escalation
- one payoff
- one share reason

No side quests.

## Slop Risks

Fail the idea if:

- the clip starts as "AI made this"
- the character has no job in the scene
- a prop appears only for a caption joke
- the voice could belong to any generic narrator
- the scene depends on flashy boxes instead of behavior
- the payoff is a CTA instead of a moment
- the format copies a creator instead of learning from structure
