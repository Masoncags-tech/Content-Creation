# QA Gates

A clip is not audience-ready until it clears these gates.

## Gate 1: First Two Seconds

- Something physical happens immediately.
- The first frame is not idle.
- The premise is visible before captions explain it.
- The hook is specific, not abstract motion.

## Gate 2: Scene Reality

- The clip could plausibly be filmed or screen-recorded by a real creator.
- Lighting and camera language stay consistent.
- The set does not reset between shots.
- The performer has a clear reason to speak.

## Gate 3: Transition Logic

- Every cut has a story reason.
- The first frame after each cut adds new information.
- Inserts cover continuity instead of creating confusion.
- No flash/zoom blur is used to hide a broken cut.

## Gate 4: Native Audio

- Voice sounds like it belongs in the room.
- Room tone starts before speech.
- Foley matches visible actions.
- Mouth/body movement roughly matches the line energy.
- The clip does not rely on a loose voiceover.

## Gate 5: Generated Media Defects

Fail the clip if it has:

- warped hands
- broken faces
- static first frame
- readable fake text
- text morphing
- fake logos
- bad end-card spelling
- UI that looks generated instead of designed

## Gate 6: Brand/Claims

- No fake metrics.
- No fake clients.
- No guaranteed ROI.
- No unscoped "fully autonomous" claims.
- No borrowed character IP, catchphrases, voices, or costume reads.

## Gate 7: Share Reason

The viewer should be able to say one of:

- "This is me."
- "This is my boss."
- "This is my business."
- "This is the exact problem."
- "That line is funny."

If the only reason to watch is that it was made with AI, it fails.
